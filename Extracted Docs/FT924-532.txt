A Texas jury awarded Dollars 25m ( Pounds 16.4m ) in damages yesterday to a Houston woman who said silicone-gel breast implants caused an autoimmune disease after rupturing inside her , Reuter reports from Houston . Pamela Johnson won the award against Medical Engineering , a subsidiary of Bristol-Myers Squibb , after claiming silicone leaked from two sets of implants , attacked her immune system and caused various illnesses , forcing her to have a partial mastectomy . Bristol-Myers Squibb , whose stock fell Dollars 3.125 to Dollars 68.125 after the ruling , plans an appeal .
