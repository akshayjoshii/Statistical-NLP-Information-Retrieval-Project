US and North Korean officials , meeting in New York , agreed yesterday on a date for the two countries to resume negotiations on their nuclear dispute , agencies report . The date , during the first week of August , is expected to be announced today after it has been confirmed by top officials in Washington and Pyongyang . The negotiations , which began in Geneva on July 8 , were broken off after only one day because of the death of North Korean leader Kim Il-sung . The US is offering diplomatic and economic concessions if North Korea abandons any plans to build nuclear weapons .
