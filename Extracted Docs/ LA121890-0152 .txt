Blood tests confirmed that 12 people who died during a religious ceremony led by a self-styled spiritualist suffered carbon monoxide poisoning , Baja California police officials said . Blood samples from three of the victims revealed lethal levels of carboxyhemoglobin , which carried carbon monoxide rather than oxygen to the victims ' organs , said Miguel Pallares , a forensic chemist with the Baja California state police . `` The tests showed high enough levels to kill these people , Pallares said . Baja California Assistant Atty . Gen. Victor Vazquez Fernandez also said tissue and blood tests indicated that the victims had not consumed alcohol or another toxic substance as first suspected , the official news agency Notimex reported .
