In response to `` Gorbachev Clearly Deserved It '' ( editorial , Oct. 16 ) : Giving Gorbachev a Nobel Prize for his contribution to world peace is rather like rewarding a eunuch for exercising sexual restraint . One wonders what Gorbachev 's contribution to the cause of peace would have been had he not been busy dealing with military defeat in Afghanistan , economic ruin at home , nationalism and ethnic unrest in the Soviet republics and the serious political challenges of Boris Yeltsin . The mere absence of the usual amount of mischief in a Soviet leader ought not to be mistaken for the good works of a peacemaker . ROBERT B. MOONEY , Covina
