Access to international stock market quotes , financial data and business news is now available via the Internet at considerably lower cost than most online services . Quote . Com , a service established by a Nevada company of the same name , lists 23 exchanges in Europe and North America . Users can access quotes for stocks , mutual funds , commodities , futures and precious metals . As part of the basic service offered by Quote . Com for Dollars 9.95 ( Pounds 6.40 ) per month , users can store a portfolio of up to 50 listed financial instruments and will receive , via E-Mail , a close-of-trading day report . During the day , they may receive E-Mail alerts if their set price limit alarms are triggered . Access to London exchanges or European exchanges costs Dollars 19.95 per month . 'The Internet gives companies the ability to reach the world market with very little difficulty , ' says Chris Cooper , president of Quote . Com . While taking advantage of the extensive reach of the Internet , Quote . Com has avoided the infrastructure and software costs generally associated with creating a new online service . Quote . Com 's Internet server can be accessed using four connection methods : telnet ( telnet quote . com ) , E-Mail ( info @ quote . com ) , direct Internet connection on a World Wide Web browser ( http : //www . quote . com ) or via file transfer protocol ( ftp . quote . com ) . Voice of America , the international radio broadcasting system funded by the US government , this week began digital audio newscasts via the Internet . The 10-minute newscasts are available in 15 languages to computer users worldwide , 24 hours a day . The digitised signals can be converted into sound by most PCs .
